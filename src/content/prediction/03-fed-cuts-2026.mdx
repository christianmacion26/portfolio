Event contracts imply 42¢ that the Fed delivers ≥ 100bp of cuts in 2026.
My cross-sectional momentum model, fitted on the past six easing cycles,
puts fair probability closer to 51¢. Two reasons the market underweights
the cut path: (1) OIS traders price to the dot-plot, which embeds SEP
median optimism, whereas realized cuts historically run ~25bp below the
initial SEP; (2) labor-market cooling is now in the 90th percentile of
the last 40 years' deceleration rate, which is a stronger predictor than
the dot-plot of forward cuts.
