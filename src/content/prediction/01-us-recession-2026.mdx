The market-implied probability of a US recession by Q4 2026 sits at 18¢. My
model-implied probability is roughly 25¢, giving a 7¢ edge over the implied
price. The edge is driven by yield-curve persistence (the 2s10s has been
un-inverted for less than 12 months, the historical median lead-time on
forward recession windows is 14-22 months), combined with the Sahm-rule
indicator drifting toward its first-decile threshold if the next two nonfarm
payroll prints come in below 80k. The view is binary and mechanically
falsifiable by the next four payroll releases.
